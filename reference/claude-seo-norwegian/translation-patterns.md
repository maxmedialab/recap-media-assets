## 2. CTA Playbook — Mapped by Commitment Level

Norwegian CTAs are calibrated to the commitment level of the action. Using the
wrong verb signals that you don't understand the Norwegian buyer's comfort zone.

### Primary CTAs (service inquiry — low commitment)

| Norwegian | When to use |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Ta kontakt** | General service inquiry. THE default B2B CTA. |
| **La oss ta en prat** | Booking an initial conversation. Warmer, relationship-oriented. |
| **La oss ta en uforpliktende prat** | The gold standard for "free consultation." "Uforpliktende" (non-binding) removes pressure without using the word "gratis" (free). |
| **Send oss en melding** | Softer alternative for contact forms. |
| **Snakk med oss** | Informal, good for footer or secondary CTAs. |

**"Ta kontakt" vs "Kontakt oss":** "Ta kontakt" implies the reader proactively
"takes" contact — more dynamic in Norwegian. "Kontakt oss" is a static command.
Always prefer "Ta kontakt."

### Transactional CTAs (clear purchase/booking)

| Norwegian | When to use |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Bestill nå** | Buying tickets, booking fixed packages. |
| **Book et møte** | Scheduling a meeting. "Book" is an accepted anglicism in Oslo. |
| **Book tid i kalenderen** | More specific meeting booking. |
| **Be om tilbud** | Request a quote. |
| **Be om et pristilbud** | Request a price quote (more specific). |

### Educational/navigation CTAs

| Norwegian | When to use |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Les mer** | Moving to a detail page. |
| **Se alle mulighetene** | Browsing options/packages. |
| **Se pakker / Se priser** | Navigating to pricing. |
| **Meld deg på** | Newsletter or webinar sign-up. |
| **Kom i gang** | Starting a trial or process. |

### The "free consultation" — Norwegian equivalents

NEVER translate literally as "Gratis konsultasjon." Use these instead:

1. **"La oss ta en uforpliktende prat"** — Non-binding chat. Best for agencies.
2. **"Skal vi ta en kaffe?"** — "Shall we have a coffee?" Even in B2B, this
   signals low-pressure, informal professional meeting. Works for local Oslo.
3. **"Få en gratis analyse"** — "Get a free analysis." If offering specific
   deliverable, "gratis" is OK paired with a concrete thing.

### Urgency — how to do it without pressure

| English pressure tactic | Norwegian professional alternative |
|------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| "Don't miss out!" | ❌ Don't use |
| "Limited spots available!" | "Begrenset antall plasser" (state factually, no exclamation) |
| "Book now before it's too late!" | ❌ Don't use |
| "Early bird pricing ends Friday" | "Tidligbooking-pris gjelder til fredag" |
| "Plan ahead for Q4 events" | "Vær tidlig ute med planleggingen" |
| "Last chance to register" | "Siste frist for påmelding: [date]" (deadline, not "last chance") |

## 3. Heading Patterns

Norwegian headings are more descriptive than English ones. Where English might
use "Event Excellence," Norwegian uses a full clause with the benefit at the end.

### H1 — Benefit + Category

| English | Norwegian |
|---------|-----------|
| "Event Photography Oslo" | "Eventfotografering i Oslo" |
| "Your Event, Captured" | "Innhold fra arrangementet ditt som faktisk blir brukt" |
| "Professional Event Coverage" | "Profesjonell dekning av konferanser og messer" |
| "Footage That Actually Gets Used" | "Innhold som faktisk blir brukt" |

### H2 — Action-oriented section labels

| English | Norwegian |
|---------|-----------|
| "Our Services" | "Dette er vi gode på" or "Våre tjenester" |
| "Why Choose Us" | "Derfor velger kundene oss" (more evidence-oriented) |
| "How It Works" | "Slik jobber vi" |
| "What We Deliver" | "Dette får du" |
| "Featured Work" | "Utvalgte prosjekter" |
| "What Our Clients Say" | "Det sier kundene" |

### H3 — Specific service labels

Use compound nouns: "Konseptutvikling," "Tekstproduksjon," "Eventfotografering"

### Overline / eyebrow text

Small uppercase labels above headings. Always in Norwegian:
- "TJENESTER" (Services)
- "REFERANSER" (References/Cases)
- "NYHETER" (News)
- "OM OSS" (About Us)
- "PRISER" (Pricing)
- "SÅ ENKELT ER DET" (That's how easy it is)
- "SLIK JOBBER VI" (How we work)

## 4. Service Descriptions — The Feature-Benefit-Process Pattern

Norwegian service descriptions follow a specific loop:
1. **Define** what the service is (clear, no fluff)
2. **Explain** how it works (methodology, process)
3. **Prove** with social proof or specific deliverables

Do NOT lead with a transformative benefit ("Transform your event into...").
Norwegian readers want to understand the delivery before the promise.

### Example: English → Norwegian service description

**English:**
"Transform your event into unforgettable content. Our passionate team of
professionals delivers stunning photography and cinematic video that captures
every magical moment."

**Norwegian:**
"Vi dekker arrangementet ditt med foto og video — fra planlegging til
ferdige leveranser. Du får en dedikert kontaktperson som koordinerer
alt på dagen, og redigert materiale du kan bruke direkte i markedsføringen
etterpå."

Why this works:
- Opens with what happens concretely ("Vi dekker...")
- Explains the process ("fra planlegging til ferdige leveranser")
- Gives the client-facing benefit at the end ("bruke direkte i markedsføringen")
- No "passionate team," no "stunning," no "magical moments"
- Terminal emphasis: the real value (usable marketing material) is last

## 5. "About Us" (Om Oss) Page Structure

Norwegian B2B "Om oss" pages are grounded and chronological, not aspirational
brand narratives. They follow this structure:

1. **Origin story** — Why was the company started? Centered on a specific problem
   in the Norwegian market, not a "vision."
2. **Growth milestones** — Concrete: year founded, office moves, team size. If
   applicable: "Gaselle-bedrift" status (fast-growth award — major trust signal).
3. **Physical location** — Specific Oslo address. Norwegian B2B sites emphasize
   physical presence: "Du finner oss i [street], [area of Oslo]."
4. **Team culture** — Values described in action, not abstraction. Use terms like
   "samhold" (unity), "tverrfaglig" (interdisciplinary), "kvalitet" (quality).
5. **Human touches** — The founder's background, team photo, mention of the
   office environment ("med takterrasse og utsikt over Akerselva").

### What to avoid on Om Oss

- "We are passionate about..." → State what you do well, concretely
- "Our vision is to..." → Describe what you actually do today
- "We believe in excellence" → Show the evidence, don't declare beliefs
- Mission statements → Norwegians are skeptical of mission statements. Cut them
  or reframe as "Slik jobber vi" (how we work).

## 6. Social Proof & Testimonials

### Testimonial attribution format

Norwegian format:
```
"[Quote text]"
— Fornavn Etternavn, Stillingstittel, Bedriftsnavn
```

Always: full name + title + company. Include company logo if available.

### English testimonials from international clients

Keep in original English. This is standard Norwegian convention — translating
testimonials loses authenticity. If needed, add a small note: "Oversatt fra
engelsk:" before a translated version, but original is preferred.

### Social proof headings

| English | Norwegian |
|---------|-----------|
| "Trusted by leading companies" | Name the companies directly — more Norwegian |
| "What Our Clients Say" | "Det sier kundene" or "Hva kundene sier" |
| "X+ events covered" | "X+ arrangementer dekket" |
| "Case Studies" | "Referanser" or "Case" (anglicism is accepted) |

## 10. False Friends & Preposition Mismatches

### False friends in marketing context

| English | Norwegian "false friend" | Correct term | Why |
|---------|------------------------|--------------|-----|
| Actual | Aktuell | Faktisk / ekte | "Aktuell" means "relevant" or "current" |
| Eventually | Eventuelt | Til slutt / endelig | "Eventuelt" means "possibly" |
| Information | Informasjon | Opplysninger | "Opplysninger" is more precise for data in B2B |
| Novelty | Novelle | Nyhet | "Novelle" means "short story" |
| Comprehensive | Komprehensiv | Omfattende | "Komprehensiv" is rarely used naturally |

### Preposition mismatches

| English | Wrong Norwegian | Correct Norwegian |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| Expert at events | Ekspert hos/ved events | Ekspert på events |
| Information on services | Informasjon på tjenester | Informasjon om tjenester |
| Focus on quality | Fokus i kvalitet | Fokus på kvalitet |
| Based in Oslo | Basert på Oslo | Basert i Oslo |
| Work with clients | Jobbe for klienter | Jobbe med kunder |

## 11. Footer Legal Requirements

Norwegian B2B footer MUST contain:

1. **Org.nr.:** 9-digit organization number (e.g., "Org.nr. 928 325 261")
2. **MVA status:** Indicate VAT registration if applicable
3. **Physical address:** Specific street address (e.g., "Akersbakken 10, 0172 Oslo")
4. **Legal links:** Personvernerklæring (Privacy Policy), Cookie-erklæring
5. **Social media:** LinkedIn (most important for B2B), Instagram
6. **Contact:** E-post, telefon

### Footer text template

```
© 2026 Recap Media
MAXFILMS Strzępka | Org.nr. XXX XXX XXX
[Adresse], Oslo
Personvernerklæring | Cookie-erklæring
```

## 12. Bullet Point & List Grammar

Norwegian has strict rules for list formatting:

**Fragment lists** (features, services) — no capital, no period:
```
Vi leverer:
• lyd og lys
• scenografi
• prosjektledelse
```

**Sentence lists** (benefits, descriptions) — capital letter, period:
```
Dette får du:
• Du øker synligheten din i sosiale medier.
• Du får tilgang til vårt nettverk av leverandører.
```

Never mix the two styles in the same list.

## 13. Patterns to Avoid — Anti-pattern Library

### Literal translations that sound wrong

| English | Bad Norwegian | Good Norwegian |
|---------|---------------|----------------|
| "We are passionate about..." | "Vi er lidenskapelige om..." | State what you do concretely. Cut the passion. |
| "Take your event to the next level" | "Ta arrangementet til neste nivå" | "Få mer ut av arrangementet" |
| "We've got you covered" | "Vi har deg dekket" | "Vi tar oss av alt" |
| "Let us help you tell your story" | "La oss hjelpe deg å fortelle din historie" | "Vi dokumenterer det som skjer" |
| "Unlock the full potential" | "Lås opp det fulle potensialet" | ❌ Don't use this concept at all |
| "We're excited to..." | "Vi er glade for å..." | Just do the thing. Don't announce excitement. |
| "Best-in-class" | "Best i klassen" | ❌ Cut entirely |
| "Game-changing" | "Banebrytende" | ❌ Too hyperbolic. Use "effektiv" or "nyskapende" |
| "Results are achieved by..." | "Resultater oppnås av..." | "Vi oppnår resultater ved å..." (active voice) |

### The ceremony problem

English: "Our dedicated team of experienced professionals will work closely
with you to craft bespoke solutions tailored to your unique needs."

Norwegian: "Vi planlegger sammen, dekker arrangementet, og leverer ferdig
materiale."

Strip every sentence to its substance. If removing an adjective doesn't change
the meaning, remove it.

### Tone calibration

**Too formal** (government/legal):
"Recap Media tilbyr profesjonelle tjenester innen fotografi og videografi for
bedriftsarrangementer i Osloregionen."

**Too casual** (social media):
"Hei! Vi knipser bilder og filmer events i Oslo. Kult, ikke sant?"

**Just right** (professional peer-to-peer):
"Recap Media dekker konferanser og messer i Oslo med foto og video — fra
planlegging til ferdige leveranser."

The target register: how you'd explain your service to another professional at
an industry event. Competent, clear, no fluff, no stiffness.
